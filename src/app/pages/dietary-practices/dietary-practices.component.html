Healthy Dietary Practices for Better Diabetes Control

Maintaining a healthy diet is essential for effectively managing diabetes.

Key Dietary Guidelines

  • Ensure **food quality**.
  • Consume an **optimal quantity** of food.
  • Eat meals at **appropriate times**.
  • Have **well-balanced meals** that include all essential nutrients.

It is beneficial to establish a routine of **three main meals and two snacks** at consistent times each day.

Illustration: Ideal Plate Division for Diabetes Management

Ideal Meal Structure

  • Starchy Foods (¼ of your plate): Include rice, bread, hoppers, string hoppers, potatoes, jackfruit, sweet potatoes, breadfruit, paratha, and pittu.
    ⚠️ Limit intake and opt for unrefined rice or whole grains.
  • Vegetables & Green Leaves (½ of your plate): Fill half of your plate with vegetables and leafy greens.
    ⚠️ Moderate intake of carrots, beetroot, and pumpkin. ✅ Best options: Beans, cabbage, okra, drumsticks, bitter gourd, and leafy greens.
  • Animal Proteins (¼ of your plate): Include eggs, fish, and chicken.
    ⚠️ Limit red meat (pork, beef) and avoid fatty cuts.

Additional Dietary Tips

  • Eat fruits as snacks, not desserts.
    ✅ Half-ripened fruits, guava, and nelli are better for blood sugar control.
  • Include nonfat milk & dairy products for a well-balanced diet.
  • Water is the best beverage choice. Avoid sugar in tea or coffee.
  • Use minimal fat and limit thick coconut milk.

Limit These Foods

  • **Sugary foods:** Ice cream, puddings, sugary drinks, jams, jaggery, sauces.
  • **Processed foods** high in salt and fat.
  • **Avoid alcohol and smoking** for better overall health.
