Glycemic Index (GI) & Its Importance

The Glycemic Index (GI) is a ranking system that measures how quickly carbohydrates in food raise blood sugar levels. Foods are rated on a scale from 0 to 100, with pure glucose assigned a value of 100.

GI Categories:
  • Low GI (0-55): Slowly digested, causing a gradual rise in blood sugar (e.g., oatmeal, apples, kidney beans).
  • Medium GI (56-69): Moderately affects blood sugar (e.g., whole wheat bread, sweet potatoes).
  • High GI (70+): Rapidly increases blood sugar levels (e.g., white bread, instant mashed potatoes, white rice).
How GI Works?

When you eat carbohydrates, your body breaks them down into glucose, which enters the bloodstream. Foods with a high GI are quickly absorbed, leading to rapid blood sugar spikes. In contrast, low-GI foods provide a slow and steady glucose release, helping maintain stable energy levels.
